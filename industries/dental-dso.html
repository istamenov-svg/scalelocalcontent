The Problem

Why DSO website content fails.

Most DSOs operate with a marketing team of 2–5 people managing content for 20, 50, or 200+ locations. When the fund closes a new acquisition, the marketing team is expected to stand up websites for those practices within weeks — while still managing everything else.

The result is predictable: identical content across every location with the city name swapped, or outsourced content that's generic enough to apply to any dentist anywhere. Google recognizes both patterns. Patients recognize both patterns. And the practice that just joined your platform sees no improvement in its online presence — which is often one of the key promises made during acquisition.

Dental SEO content needs to reflect the specific services each practice offers, the providers who work there, the community it serves, and the competitive landscape in that specific market. A general dentist in suburban Phoenix faces different search competition than a cosmetic dentist in downtown Miami.

Typical DSO page taxonomy

  • Homepage with practice overview
  • About the practice & team page
  • Individual provider bio pages
  • General dentistry services
  • Cosmetic dentistry services
  • Orthodontics / Invisalign
  • Pediatric dentistry (if applicable)
  • Emergency dental care
  • Dental implants / oral surgery
  • Insurance & financing
  • New patient information
  • Contact & location
